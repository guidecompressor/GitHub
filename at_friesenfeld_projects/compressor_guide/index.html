Installation Guide for Car Compressors

Installation Instructions

  1. Professional Installation Required: The entire installation and dismantling must be carried out by a trained professional.
  2. Preparation:
    • Use tools prescribed by the manufacturer.
    • Use approved lifting equipment and secure the vehicle properly.
    • Disconnect and/or isolate stored energy (power supply and air pressure release).
  3. System Check: Ensure the entire air suspension system is tight before installing the compressor and relay. Use a leak spray or soapy water to detect leaks.
  4. Relay Replacement: Always use a new relay and replace it before putting the new compressor into operation.
  5. Hose Inspection: Examine the old intake hose for leaks and porosity. Replace it if damaged, as well as the intake filter.
  6. Power Connection: Connect the power supply via the vehicle's original connector to avoid reverse polarity and damage.
  7. Rubber Parts and Springs: Replace rubber parts and springs to avoid loud running noises from the compressor.
  8. Post-Installation Check: Immediately after installing the compressor, check the entire system for tightness. Perform an annual leak test.
  9. Noise Check: Start the vehicle and the air suspension system and pay attention to the compressor running noises. The compressor should shut off after 20-30 seconds, indicating that the system is ready for use.
  10. Nitrogen Injection: This is an air suspension compressor with an internal circulation structure. After installation, inject 16-17 MPA nitrogen gas through the connection into the gas storage tank.

Safety Rules

  • Follow vehicle-specific installation instructions.
  • Use prescribed tools and approved lifting equipment.
  • Ensure energy sources are properly isolated or disconnected.

Warranty Conditions

  1. Coverage: The warranty period is three years under normal use conditions.
  2. Exclusions: No warranty claims for compressor overheating due to faulty software sensors or electrical issues (e.g., with the relay). No warranty for water damage caused by damaged suction hose or compressor filter.
  3. Conditions: Unidentified foreign matter entering the pump body, improper installation, or failure to follow installation instructions will void the warranty. Damage caused by not following the above instructions will not be covered.

Attention: Use the appropriate shop manual for your vehicle for specific service instructions. If you lack an installation manual or sufficient skills, seek help from a qualified mechanic.
